The Fallen Kingdom of Hallownest

Deep beneath a forgotten town lies a vast, ruined kingdom — Hallownest. Once a thriving civilisation built by the enigmatic Pale King, it now lies silent, its citizens lost to a spreading infection born from a dreaming god called The Radiance. You are the Knight — a small, nameless vessel of Void — descending alone into the darkness to uncover the truth of what was sacrificed to hold this world together.

What makes Hollow Knight singular is its hand-crafted silence: 17 interconnected regions, thousands of hand-drawn frames, a haunting orchestral score, and a lore told entirely through item descriptions, faded murals, and the dying words of forgotten bugs. Nothing is explained. Everything is felt.

The Story Behind the Game

How Team Cherry Made Hollow Knight

01

Three People. One Dream.

Hollow Knight was created by Team Cherry — a tiny studio of just three people based in Adelaide, Australia: Ari Gibson (art & direction), William Pellen (programming & design), and David Kazi (business). It began as a small prototype for a 2014 game jam.

02

Kickstarter & The Community

In 2014, Team Cherry launched a Kickstarter campaign with a modest goal of $35,000 AUD. The community responded overwhelmingly — they raised over $57,000. That faith from backers gave them the runway to build something far larger than originally planned.

03

Two and a Half Years of Craft

Development stretched from 2014 to early 2017. Every frame of animation was hand-drawn. Every room was built by hand. The composer Christopher Larkin wrote a full orchestral score alone. The team worked with extraordinary discipline to build one of the largest indie games of its era.

04

Launch & Legacy

Hollow Knight released on February 24, 2017 for PC. It sold over 2.8 million copies in its first two years with zero marketing — entirely by word of mouth. It is now considered one of the greatest indie games ever made, and its sequel Silksong is one of the most anticipated games in the industry.

Thank you, Team Cherry — for proving that three people, a dream, and an obsessive love of craft can create something that millions of people will carry with them forever.
